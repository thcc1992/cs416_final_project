It is important to look at other metrics such as the mortality rate, which for the purposes of this analysis is defined as the number of deaths divided per number of cases on a given date. When coupled with other information about the pandemic, this variable can help determine how deadly a variant is and how the measures in place at a certain time helped decrease the number of deaths. For example, the mortality was the highest at the beginning of the pandemic (February 2020), before any preventive measures were in place. That prompted authorities to put the whole country under a lockdown. A few months later, the mortality was drastically reduced and remained fairly low until early 2021. That was most likely caused by the increase in gathering due to the holidays and the spread of the Delta variant, which is known to be more deadly than the previous variants. Another peak occurred around March 2022, which partially due to several states starting to be more flexible on pandemic preventive measures such as the mandatory use of face mask.
